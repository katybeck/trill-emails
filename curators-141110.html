This week at Trill we are SO excited to introduce you to our latest curator, Kimberleigh Holman, who will be scouring Boston, Cambridge, and Somerville to find us the best dance performances the city has to offer. Get to know Kim a little better, or check out some of the great options she's started us out with this week!

This weekend we also sent our fearless staffer Noah out to the "deliciously weird" Hassle Fest 6 — an annual celebration of underground music, art, and culture — and asked him to collect some of his favorite moments for your enjoyment.

Kimberleigh Holman

The Okeanos Collective presents HoverDive

Friday, November 14, at Boston University Dance Theater in Boston

If you enjoy your concert dance mixed with ocean life and the effects of climate change (seriously!), this one is for you. The Okeanos Collective, comprised of choreographer, scientist, composer and costume designer, presents this evening-length work of "multimedia dance... confronting our relationship with the ocean" which looks to be not just visually impressive but a treat for your brain and social conscience.
 
More info

Identified Moving Objects

Saturday, November 15, at Dance Complex in Cambridge

The Dance Complex is an iconic local dance institution that should be on your radar. This weekend it will present the work of young local dance makers alongside Boston big-names Margot Parsons, Prometheus Dance, Jody Weber and many more; ballet to bellydance! Need another reason to check out the show? Local jazz dance legend Adrienne Hawkins will return to the stage to perform a new solo — don't miss it!

Bowery Boston & World Music/CRASHarts Presents Agnes Obel and Jennifer Castle

Monday, November 10, at The Sinclair in Cambridge

Danish singer/songwriter Agnes Obel's music is sparse and hauntingly melancholic — piano, strings, and a hushed vocal style create the feeling of being whispered beautiful secrets.
 
More info

Bowery Boston presents An Evening with Amanda Palmer: The Art of Asking Book Tour

Tuesday, November 11, at Royale in Boston

Amanda Palmer, half of Boston punk-cabaret duo the Dresden Dolls, celebrates the release of her first book The Art of Asking on her home turf. Expect special guests, conversations about what it means to be an artist in the 21st century and a smattering of well-chosen, collaborative songs in the vein of Nico as modern-day troubadour.

Opensound: Adventures in Sound

Saturday, November 15, at Third Life Studio in Somerville

The last of the 2014 season in this avant-garde and improvisational music series (which starts up again in late January) . Tonight includes the graphic score based movement & sound collection improvisation of Inedit Ensemble, "third-stream"-ers Cloud Ludum Ensemble, performance artist Mitch Ahern, and more.

Tsons of Tsunami, Crown Larks, Lair, and Big Mess

Wednesday, November 12, at T.T. The Bears Place in Cambridge

Its a slop-tastic ad hoc affair of outside-the-lines rock experimentation that proudly resists easy categorization. Spunky surf zombies cut it up with disturbed electric loners and fizzy fuzz groovers for this mish-mash slamdance of a good time.
 
More info

Daniel Bachman, S.S. Cretins, and Soft Eyes

Thursday, November 13, at Deep Thoughts in Jamaica Plain

Mosey down to Deep Thoughts record store, the freshest vinyl peddler in the city, for a heady mix of finger picking guitar zones in two complimentary modes. Southern belle Mr. Bachman walks the path of American primitivist heroes Fahey, Basho, et al, with alacrity and aplomb, while rambunctious garage fiends SS Cretins and Soft Eyes tackle free-wheeling psychedelia from a more amped up angle.

Allston Pudding Presents: Babydriver, Doug Tuttle, ABADABAD

Friday, November 14, at Great Scott in Allston

A trio of New England jangle pop crooners awaits your hungry ears in Allston this night with pop psych seer Doug Tuttle playing the major keys of your heart, with delicate indie boppers Babydriver, and soaring West Coast nostalgists Abadabad.

Classical

Sarah Darling

Boston Baroque - Monteverdi Vespers

Friday, November 14, at Jordan Hall in Boston

How can it be that Monteverdi's ancient Vespers of 1610 — just a few years over 400 — is still one of the most stunningly complex and inspiring works out there? Blessed with a combination of mind-blowing, heart-melting, and soul-affirming moments, it is just extraordinary. Don't miss the chance to hear it unfold live; there are angels, living echoes, and revelations to apprehend!
 
More info

Obsession presented by A Far Cry

Saturday, November 15, at St. John's Church in Jamaica Plain

A Far Cry is teaming up with early music goddess Amanda Forsythe for its first all-Baroque program, and nobody knows quite what will happen! But chances are that it'll pair AFC's stylistic flexibility and vibrant energy with Amanda's radiant soprano and utter command of gesture. There'll also be some significantly funky orchestral interludes, ending with Vivaldi's rock masterpiece, La Follia.
 
More info

Mendelssohn/Wolf Chamber Series, Concert II

Sunday, November 16, at Emmanuel Church in Boston

Known for its weekly performances of Bach cantatas in a liturgical setting (this week features the iconic Wachet Auf, at the Emmanuel Church service Sunday morning) Emmanuel Music also presents wonderful chamber concerts surveying lesser-known works and composers. This Sunday, the Arneis Quartet will attack that most awesome of all Mendelssohn quartets, op 13, and David Kravitz and Roberta Anderson will sweet-talk Hugo Wolf's quirky lieder.
